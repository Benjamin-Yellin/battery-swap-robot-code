Battery-Swap Gantry — Operator Guide

Autonomous gantry that picks up and sets down robot batteries. The Raspberry Pi runs the vision + decision loop; the Arduino drives the X/Y steppers and the Z (scoop) actuator.

0. The dashboard at a glance

  • The Next step banner (below the status bar) always tells you what to do next and gives the button to do it — just follow it.
  • Controls are split into tabs: Operate (run jobs), Setup & Calibrate (one-time camera setup), and Manual (jog / GOTO / testing).
  • Status pills up top: Arduino, Homed, Cal (camera calibrated?), State, Grip, and Motion mode (GENTLE while carrying a battery, else NORMAL).

1. Startup sequence

  1. Power the Arduino (USB). It prints SYSTEM READY — this means it is alive, not that it can move yet.
  2. Power the motor PSU.
  3. Press HOME and wait for Homed: YES. Homing retracts Z, then homes X, then Y.
  4. Operation buttons stay disabled until homed.

2. Coordinate & axis conventions

  • X = along the gantry. Y = toward/away from the robot (0 = retracted). Z = scoop up/down.
  • The scoop is an upside-down "V" that cradles the battery handle from underneath.

3. Operate — Service Target Robot

The target robot's positions aren't known ahead of time, so its operations are keyed by marker ID (not slot number, the way our own carrier slots are). Each button runs its whole sequence autonomously — there's no separate "align" step.

  • PICK UP BATTERY (enter the battery's marker ID): the full staged pick-up below.
  • SET DOWN INTO SLOT (enter the slot-frame marker ID): place the carried battery.
  • alignment details (collapsible) shows live X/Z pixel error and marker tilt vs. target.

4. Pick-up flow (target robot) — staged, one button

  1. Search: returns X to 0 (and retracts Z so the camera can see), then sweeps +X until the battery marker is found.
  2. Coarse align X+Z onto the pick-up crosshair (rough — it's far away).
  3. Approach Y until the marker grows to the pre-engage width (close, just before the handle enters the scoop).
  4. Fine align X+Z again — now at the calibration depth, so it's accurate — then check orientation. If the marker is too tilted, it aborts here before committing.
  5. Engage: drive Y the last bit to the full engagement width, lift Z, retract Y.
  6. The grip switch confirms a battery is held; status warns if the scoop came back empty.

🐢 Step mode (approve each move): tick the checkbox under PICK UP BATTERY before starting. Search still runs on its own (the scoop is up — nothing to hit), but from coarse align onward every move is staged and waits for you. A bar appears at the top showing exactly what it wants to do (e.g. "move X +47 steps, x_err=120px"); press APPROVE (or the A key) to run that one move, RESUME AUTO to let the rest finish on its own, or ABORT to cancel. Use it to watch the logic and stop the arm before it can bump into anything.

5. Set-down flow (target robot)

Carrying a ~30 kg battery is a pendulum, so the whole op runs under a gentle motion profile (reduced accel/speed — the "Motion mode" pill reads GENTLE). Unlike a pick-up there's no handle to thread, so it never stages a Y engage and never aligns in Z.

  1. Raise Z fully first — battery to carry height (max ground clearance, shortest pendulum) before any horizontal move.
  2. Find the slot flag marker, then align over the slot in X and Y only (Y from the marker width). Z stays retracted.
  3. Lower straight down in small steps, re-centering X between pulses, until the grip switch releases — the battery's weight has transferred to the slot (seated). The firmware stops the lowering pulse the instant that happens. Backstops: the calibrated seated pose and a relative travel guard (for a never-seats jam).
  4. Retract Y to slide the scoop off. The scoop never raises Z to disengage (that would lift the battery back out).

6. Our Carrier Slots

Our own slots are at fixed positions with no markers (no vision). Pick-up dead-reckons (with an optional vision refine if the pick-up profile is calibrated). Set-down re-homes X/Y with the battery first — a fresh switch reference, since open-loop steps drift worst under a swinging load — then dead-reckons to the slot and lowers to the absolute seat depth, stopping early the instant the grip switch releases. Keep the traverse path clear; it runs gentle (loaded) too.

7. Setup & Calibrate — Camera Calibration (two profiles)

  • Pick-up references the battery-case marker; Set-down references the slot flag marker. Different references, calibrated separately. Each captures two poses.
  • Pick-up ① Aim point: at the close pre-engage pose (scoop right at the handle, just before it enters), marker centered — stores the crosshair, the marker's outline + orientation (the per-profile tilt target), and the pre-engage width. ② Engage depth: at full engagement, stores the width the final Y approach stops at.
  • Set-down ① Over-slot pose: with the battery held at full Z-retract, parked over the slot — stores the X aim and the Y depth (width) the descent starts from; this one capture makes set-down alignment-ready. ② Seated pose (optional but recommended): lower until seated, then capture — stores where the marker sits when seated, used as the descent guard + a placement check.

7b. Setup — Scoop Z Heights (required for Our-Carrier-Slot operations)

The Our-Carrier-Slot Pick Up / Set Down buttons need two lab-measured Z travel times (the Z actuator has no encoder, so distance is tracked as motion time). These are only for our own carrier, where the geometry is fixed; the target robot's handle height varies per visit and is found live by vision, so it needs nothing here.

  • ① Handle depth — from fully retracted (top), how long the scoop lowers until it reaches handle height (where it cradles a battery handle). This is the seat depth: pick-up lowers to here, and set-down stops here if the grip switch hasn't already released.
  • ② Clear-the-slot lift — from handle height, how long it lifts a cradled battery until it's fully clear of the slot bracing (so Y can retract without snagging).

Click ▶ Measure on a row for a guided stopwatch: position the scoop, press START, watch it move, and press CAPTURE at the right point — the elapsed time is filled in for you (you can also type values directly). Until both are set, the Our-Slot buttons stay disabled and the Setup tab shows a badge. The target set-down instead lowers until the grip switch signals seated, backed by a fixed relative travel guard so a jam can't crush the battery.

7c. Setup — Alignment Tolerances (advanced)

How close counts as "aligned" before the robot commits, per operation (pick-up vs set-down), for each of X, Z, width (px) and roll/pitch/yaw (°). Bigger = more lenient. Leave at defaults unless tuning. The alignment details panel shows each live error against its tolerance, and the active marker outline turns green only when position and width and orientation are all within tolerance.

Note on "width": it's the marker's size as √(area), not one edge — so it stays an honest distance measure even when the marker is rolled or tilted.

8. Marker color legend & on-feed overlays

Green = active marker, in tolerance · Red = active, out of tolerance · Grey = other detected marker (dimmed, no telemetry). Use Marker Labels (Setup tab) to name any ID so it's annotated on the feed.

Why is it red? Under the active marker, three chips — L/R (left-right), U/D (up-down) and Depth — each turn green (in tolerance) or red (off), so you can see exactly which term is blocking the commit. Below them, the Tilt line reads Tilt OK when square-on, or names each off-axis with its acceptable range (e.g. Yaw 16deg (ok: -10 to 10)) so the number explains itself. While off-position, an arrow points from the marker toward the crosshair (amber when close, red when far) and disappears once L/R + U/D are aligned.

Depth gauge (right edge, shown only while driving in): the marker's width is the depth proxy (closer = wider). A pointer rises to the current depth; the green engaged band is the stop depth (± tolerance) you drive in to, and the line-up tick is the pre-engage depth. The pointer turns green once inside the band.

Phase banner (top-left): the running op's stages left→right — green = done, bright = active, grey = upcoming — so you can track progress without reading the status line.

Calibration outlines on the feed: cyan = pick-up, magenta = set-down. Each profile shows two captured poses: the thick "line-up" outline (SET XZ REF) and the thin "engaged" outline (SET WIDTH REF), so you can verify both calibrations at a glance. The aim crosshair (+) shows the saved aim point — faint for each calibrated profile when idle; during an operation the active profile's crosshair gains a dashed box = its left-right / up-down position tolerance (so position tolerance is shown by the box, depth tolerance by the green band, and tilt tolerance by the range in the Tilt line).

9. Manual tab — Position Control, Jog & Track

GOTO moves to absolute X/Y/Z (mm) with one GO; the fields track the live position, so only the axis you change actually moves. Accel sets the motion acceleration and Speed the max X/Y travel speed — both are global (jog and automated sequences share them). Lower the speed for more stepper torque against friction/load (steppers make peak torque at low speed); the firmware clamps it to 10–200 steps/s. Manual Jog nudges each axis by the configured step/pulse; arrow keys jog XY, PgUp/PgDn jog Z (Z UP = retract, Z DN = extend). Track Marker continuously centers a marker for testing — it never picks up.

To clear a calibration, use the CLEAR button on its profile in the Setup tab.

10. E-STOP

E-STOP halts all motion immediately (including homing). After an E-STOP the robot is no longer homed — press HOME again before operating.
